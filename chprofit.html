CH-Profit and Loss
The amount paid to purchase an article or the price at which an article is made, is known as its cost price and is abbreviated as C.P. The price at which an article is sold, is called its selling price and is abbreviated as S.P.
PROFIT
If the selling price is greater than the cost price, the difference between the selling price and cost price is called profit.

Profit = S.P.-C.P.
LOSS
If the selling price of an article is less than the cost price, the difference between the cost price and the selling price is called loss.

Loss = C.P.- S.P.
MARKED PRICE
If the selling price of an article is less than the cost price, the difference between the cost price and the selling price is called loss.

COST AND SELLING PRICE
The amount paid to purchase an article or the price at which an article is made, is known as its cost price and is abbreviated as C.P. The price at which an article is sold, is called its selling price and is abbreviated as S.P.
DISCOUNT
In order to increase the sale or clear the old stock, sometimes the shopkeepers offer a certain percentage of rebate on the marked price. This rebate is called discount.

Discount = M.P.- S.P.

EXAMPLES
Example 1: By selling 42 oranges, a person loses a sum equal to the selling price of eight oranges.
Find the loss per cent.

Solution:
Let the S.P. of 1 orange= 1

S.P. of 42 oranges = (1x42) = 42

Loss = S.P. of 8 oranges

= 1x8= 8

= 42 + 8 = 50

Example 2: The marked price of a T.V. is 32.500. After allowing a 20% Diwali discount to the customer, a shopkeeper still makes a profit of 30%. Find the cost price of the TV.

Solution:
Here, M.P. of the T.V.= 32,500

Discount = 20%

Hence, S.P. of the T.V. = M.P. - Discount

= 32,500 - 6,500 = 26,000

Profit = 30%
